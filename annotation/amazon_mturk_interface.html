In this task, you will see one conversation history between two speakers and one response. Your job is to highlight text that you believe are the causes of the response. You can understand the cause of response is this way "because of these texts, the speaker makes this response" or "without these texts, making this response is groundless".
How to do this task:
  1. Read dialogue history and response carefully.
  2. Select text that you think is the cause of the response.
  3. Click the label on the right side to highlight text.
  4. Click “submit” on the bottom right corner to submit your task.
  5. To remove a label from highlighted text, choose the X next to the abbreviated label name on the highlighted text.
  6. If you think there is no cause of this response, click “No entities to label” on the bottom right corner, and submit it.
  7. You can select all of a previously highlighted text, but not a portion of it.

There are some examples to help you better understand our task.
Example 1:

Speaker 1: I've decided to visit Mexico next week for a month. It will give me some good content for my blog.
Speaker 2: Nice. Will you be staying in one place or moving all around?
Speaker 1: I can't wait! I'm going to pack in as much as I can and will travel all over. I'll start on the west coast by the beaches!
Response: I've never been myself but my cousins have and their pictures looked amazing. Make sure you bring extra chargers and memory cards so you have enough space. Is your blog about travel?
Why annotate these text:

  1. Speaker1 mentions (s)he will travel to Mexico, which is the cause of "I've never been myself but my cousins have and their pictures looked amazing".
  2. Speaker1 mentions (s)he is going to pack, which is the cause of "Make sure you bring extra chargers and memory cards so you have enough space".
  3. Speaker1 mentions (s)he visit Mexico for blog, which is the cause of "Is your blog about travel?".
